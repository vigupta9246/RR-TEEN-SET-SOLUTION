Prefab Warehouse in Delhi NCR: Everything You Need to Know (2026)

Planning to build a prefabricated warehouse in Delhi NCR? This complete guide covers cost, sizes, timeline, key features, and how to choose the right contractor.

Delhi NCR is one of India's biggest logistics and manufacturing hubs. Whether you're running an e-commerce operation, a manufacturing unit, or a cold storage facility — a prefab warehouse is often the fastest and most cost-effective solution.

In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know before building a prefab warehouse in Delhi, Noida, Gurgaon, Faridabad, or Ghaziabad.

💡 Quick Fact: A prefab warehouse can be built 3–4x faster than an RCC building and costs 30–40% less — making it the preferred choice for 70%+ of businesses in Delhi NCR's industrial areas.

What is a Prefabricated Warehouse?

A prefab warehouse is a steel-framed structure where components (columns, beams, purlins, roofing) are fabricated off-site and assembled at your location. The result is a weather-proof, high-clearance, large-span storage structure that's ready in weeks — not months.

Key Features of a Good Prefab Warehouse

1. Clear Height

For a functional warehouse, minimum 7m clear height is recommended. E-commerce and racking systems need 9–12m. Forklift operations need at least 6m. Always plan your height based on operations — not just current use.

2. Clear Span (No Middle Columns)

Middle columns obstruct movement of goods, forklifts, and large vehicles. A good prefab warehouse should have 20–40m clear span depending on size. Our PEB structures offer up to 90m clear span.

3. Loading Dock & Entry Points

Think about how trucks and vehicles will load/unload. Dock levelers, wide sliding doors (12×12 ft minimum), and proper apron space outside are essential.

4. Ventilation

Delhi NCR summers are brutal. Turbo ventilators (₹8/sq.ft add-on) keep the warehouse temperature manageable and prevent humidity damage to stored goods.

5. Fire Safety Provisions

Plan for fire exit points, ventilation windows, and spacing for sprinkler systems if storing flammable goods. Your contractor should factor this into the design.

How Long Does a Prefab Warehouse Take to Build?

  • Small (up to 3,000 sq.ft): 15–20 working days
  • Medium (3,000–10,000 sq.ft): 20–35 working days
  • Large (10,000+ sq.ft): 35–55 working days

Compare this to RCC construction which takes 4–8 months for the same size. That's why businesses in Noida, Gurgaon, and Faridabad prefer prefab warehouses when speed matters.

Permissions Needed for Warehouse in Delhi NCR

This varies by area, but typically you'll need:

  • Land use permission (industrial or commercial zone)
  • Building plan approval from local authority (MCD, NMMC, etc.)
  • Fire NOC (for large warehouses)
  • Environment clearance (for certain industries)

Important: Always check if your land is in an industrial zone before starting construction. Our team can guide you through this process.

5 Things to Check When Hiring a Warehouse Contractor

  1. GST registration and proper invoicing capability
  2. Portfolio of similar-size warehouse projects in Delhi NCR
  3. Use of IS-grade certified steel (ask for material test reports)
  4. Written timeline and payment schedule
  5. Post-construction support and warranty terms

⚠️ Red flag: Any warehouse contractor in Delhi NCR quoting below ₹165/sq.ft is likely cutting corners on material quality. This will result in leakage, structural weakness, or complete failure within 5–7 years.

Conclusion

A prefab warehouse is the smart choice for Delhi NCR businesses that need large storage space quickly and cost-effectively. With the right contractor, proper planning, and IS-grade materials, your warehouse will serve you for 20–30 years without major maintenance.
