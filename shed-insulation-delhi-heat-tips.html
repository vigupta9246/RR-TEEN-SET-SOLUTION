How to Keep Your Tin Shed Cool in Delhi's Extreme Summer Heat

A tin shed in Delhi NCR can reach 60°C inside during summer without proper insulation. This guide shows you exactly how to reduce shed temperature by 10–15°C — using proven methods that work in our climate.

Ask anyone with a tin shed in Delhi, Noida, or Gurgaon — summer is brutal. Temperatures outside hit 45°C, but inside an uninsulated tin shed, it can feel like 55–60°C. Workers cannot function, goods get damaged, and machines overheat.

The good news: with the right insulation and ventilation design, you can reduce inside temperature by 10–15°C — making your shed comfortable and productive even in peak Delhi summer.

💡 Science behind it: GI metal sheets are excellent heat conductors — they absorb sunlight rapidly and radiate it inward. The solution is to either stop heat from entering (insulation) or remove hot air as it builds up (ventilation). Best results come from doing both.

Method 1: Insulation Sheets (Most Effective)

Insulation sheets are placed below the GI roofing. They create a thermal barrier that blocks up to 60–70% of radiant heat. Types available in Delhi NCR:

a) Bubble Wrap Insulation (HDPE + Foil)

Most affordable — ₹15–18/sq.ft add-on. Reduces roof temperature by 8–12°C. Best for budget-conscious projects like small factories, godowns, and workshops. Lifespan: 8–12 years.

b) Sandwich Panel (PUF Insulated)

Premium option — ₹280–380/sq.ft for complete roofing. Reduces inside temperature by 12–18°C. Used in cold storage, food processing, and high-value manufacturing. Also provides sound insulation. Lifespan: 20+ years.

c) Rock Wool / Glass Wool Blankets

Industrial grade — ₹22–30/sq.ft. Used in industrial sheds where fireproofing is also needed. Reduces temperature by 10–14°C. Best for large factories in Noida and Gurgaon industrial areas.

Method 2: Turbo Ventilators (Zero Electricity)

Turbo ventilators are wind-powered rotating exhaust units installed on the roof ridge. As wind turns the blades, they create low pressure inside and pull hot air out — continuously, without any electricity.

  • Cost: ₹8/sq.ft add-on (installed)
  • Recommendation: 1 unit per 400–500 sq.ft of roof area
  • Effectiveness: Removes 2,000–3,000 cubic meters of hot air per hour
  • Temperature reduction: 4–8°C additional, combined with insulation
  • Zero running cost — 100% wind powered

Method 3: Ridge Vents

A continuous ridge vent (opening along the peak of the roof) allows hot air to escape naturally as it rises. This is a passive ventilation method that works well combined with low inlet ventilation (side windows or open sides).

Method 4: Side Wall Ventilation

For agricultural sheds and workshops, open side walls with GI wire mesh (rather than full GI sheet cladding) provide excellent cross-ventilation. This is the most effective method for poultry sheds — but not suitable for dust-sensitive manufacturing.

Method 5: Orientation and Design (Plan It Right From the Start)

Before construction, the shed orientation significantly affects heat buildup:

  • North-south orientation: Walls face east and west — reduces direct sun exposure on long walls
  • Overhang design: Extended eaves by 1–1.5m shade the walls from afternoon sun
  • Roof slope: Steeper roof (1:5 vs 1:10) allows faster hot air movement upward
  • White/light color roofing: Light grey or white GI sheets reflect 20–30% more heat than dark green or red

Conclusion
