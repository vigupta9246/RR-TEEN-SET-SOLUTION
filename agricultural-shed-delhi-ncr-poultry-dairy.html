Agricultural Shed in Delhi NCR: Best Options for Poultry & Dairy Farms

Whether you run a poultry farm, dairy unit, or crop storage operation near Delhi NCR, choosing the right shed design can make or break your productivity. Here's what you need to know.

Agricultural sheds are different from industrial sheds. Animals need ventilation. Crops need humidity control. Equipment needs space. And all of this needs to work in Delhi NCR's extreme climate — 45°C summers, heavy monsoon, and cold winters.

In this guide, we'll walk you through the best shed options for farms around Delhi, Noida, Gurgaon, Faridabad, and Ghaziabad.

Types of Agricultural Sheds We Build in Delhi NCR

1. Poultry Farm Sheds

Poultry sheds need specific design for bird health and productivity. Key requirements:

  • Minimum 12–14 ft height for proper air circulation
  • Wide span (20–30m) with no middle columns
  • Turbo ventilators — 1 per 500 sq.ft minimum
  • GI wire mesh side walls for natural ventilation
  • Corrugated GI sheet roofing with insulation layer
  • No direct sunlight entry from east-west sides

2. Dairy Farm Sheds

Cattle comfort directly affects milk production. A well-designed dairy shed should have:

  • Cross-ventilation design (doors on both sides)
  • Concrete flooring with drainage channels
  • Height of at least 12 ft for air movement
  • Shade netting or insulation on roof
  • Separate areas for milking, feeding, and resting

3. Crop & Equipment Storage Sheds

For crop storage, grain storage, or agricultural equipment, key needs are:

  • Weatherproof — no leakage even in heavy monsoon
  • Rodent-resistant side walls (metal or concrete)
  • Wide entry door for tractors and vehicles
  • Good ventilation to prevent moisture buildup

The Most Critical Factor: Ventilation

Delhi NCR heat is the biggest enemy of agricultural productivity. Without proper ventilation:

  • Poultry mortality increases by 20–30% in summer
  • Milk production drops by 15–25% in high heat
  • Stored crops develop fungus and spoil faster

Ventilation Solutions We Recommend

  • Turbo ventilators: Most cost-effective — ₹8/sq.ft add-on, zero electricity
  • Ridge vents: Along the roof ridge for upward hot air escape
  • Open side walls: GI wire mesh on sides for maximum airflow
  • Insulation sheets: Reduces roof temperature by 10–15°C — ₹15/sq.ft

Common Mistakes Farmers Make When Building Sheds

  1. Orientation error: Building shed in east-west direction causes direct sun heat. Always north-south orientation.
  2. Low height: Many farmers build 8–10 ft high to save cost. Wrong — this traps heat and increases mortality.
  3. No drainage: Water pooling inside a dairy shed causes disease. Plan drainage channels into the flooring design.
  4. Cheap roofing: Thin GI sheets (0.35mm) warp and leak within 2 years in Delhi NCR weather. Use 0.45mm minimum.
  5. Ignoring expansion: Plan for future expansion in the structural design from day one.
