What Affects the Cost of a Tin Shed in Delhi NCR?

Not all sheds cost the same. Here are the main factors that change the final price:

1. Size of the Shed

Larger sheds are usually cheaper per square foot because of bulk material usage. A 5,000 sq.ft industrial shed will cost less per sqft than a 500 sq.ft residential shed.

2. Type of Steel Used

IS-grade certified steel (which we use at R.R Teen Set Solution) costs slightly more than substandard material — but lasts 3x longer. Always insist on IS-grade steel and ask for the certificate.

3. Height of the Shed

Taller sheds (above 12 feet) require heavier columns and more complex fabrication, which increases cost. Standard residential sheds are 10–12 ft; industrial sheds go up to 15–20 ft.

4. Location in Delhi NCR

Distance affects transportation cost for materials. Projects in central Delhi may cost slightly less than remote areas of Ghaziabad or Faridabad.

5. Add-on Features

  • Insulation sheets: +₹15/sq.ft
  • Turbo ventilators: +₹8/sq.ft
  • Side windows: +₹12/sq.ft
  • Sliding door (per door): +₹20,000 fixed
  • Anti-rust coating: +₹25/sq.ft
  • Color-coated GI sheets: +₹10/sq.ft

How to Save Money on Your Tin Shed Without Compromising Quality

1. Plan the Size Correctly

Over-building wastes money. Under-building causes problems later. Plan your shed size carefully — think about future expansion too.

2. Choose the Right Material Grade

Don't go for the cheapest option. IS-grade steel costs 10–15% more but lasts 3x longer. The savings over 10 years are massive.

3. Get Multiple Quotes — But Verify Them

Get 3–4 quotes. But be careful — lowest price often means lower quality material or hidden charges. Always check GST registration and past projects.

4. Avoid Peak Season

Rates can be slightly higher during high-demand months (Jan–March). Booking in advance for April–June delivery can save 5–8%.

⚠️ Warning: If a contractor quotes less than ₹150/sq.ft for a tin shed in Delhi NCR, be very careful. This usually means substandard steel (below IS grade), no anti-rust coating, or missing structural elements that will fail within 3–5 years.
