Tin Shed & Roof Repair in Delhi NCR

Leaking roof, rusted columns, or sagging purlins? Most shed and roofing problems can be repaired without a full rebuild. Here's what usually goes wrong and what it costs to fix.

If your tin shed roof is leaking, sagging, or rusting, you don't always need a full replacement. Most shed and roofing problems in Delhi NCR can be fixed at a fraction of new-construction cost — if diagnosed correctly and repaired with the right materials.

R.R Teen Set Solution handles shed and roof repair calls across Delhi NCR every week, from small leakage fixes to major structural repairs after storm damage. Here's a practical guide to what usually goes wrong, and what it costs to fix.

💡 Key Insight: Roof leakage is the most common repair call we get, and 70% of the time it's caused by damaged screw washers or unsealed overlaps — not the roofing sheet itself. This means many "leaking roof" problems cost far less to fix than people expect.

Most Common Tin Shed & Roof Problems We Repair

1. Roof Leakage

Usually caused by perished rubber washers on screws, gaps at sheet overlaps, or physical damage from storms or falling branches. We locate the exact entry point (often not where the water appears inside) and reseal or replace the affected section.

2. Rust & Corrosion

Old sheds — especially those over 8–10 years old without maintenance — develop rust at base plates, welded joints, and screw points. If caught early, this is a surface treatment job. If neglected for years, structural members may need reinforcement or replacement.

3. Sagging or Bent Purlins

Overloading the roof (common when people use shed roofs for storage) or age-related fatigue can cause GI purlins to sag. This is a structural repair — we add supporting members or replace the affected purlins.

4. Storm & Wind Damage

Delhi NCR's pre-monsoon dust storms can lift or tear roofing sheets, especially on older sheds with worn fasteners. We handle emergency repairs to prevent further water damage until a full fix is scheduled.

5. Old Roof Replacement

Sometimes a roof has simply reached end of life — sheets are too rusted or brittle to patch further. We assess whether a partial re-sheet or full roof replacement makes more financial sense.

Repair or Replace? A Simple Way to Decide

As a rough guide: if less than 25% of the roof area is affected and the structural frame (columns and beams) is sound, repair is almost always the cheaper and faster option. If more than half the roof is failing, or the frame itself shows rust-through or bending, replacement is usually more cost-effective long-term than repeated patch repairs.

Signs You Need Repair Now, Not Later

  • Water stains or active dripping inside the shed during rain
  • Visible rust streaks running down walls or columns
  • Sheets that flex or rattle noticeably in wind
  • Any visible bending or sagging in the roof line

Delaying repair on any of these almost always increases the eventual cost — water damage spreads to more sheets and accelerates rust once it starts.
